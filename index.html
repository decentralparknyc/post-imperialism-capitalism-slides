Part I — Post-Capitalism

What Is Capitalism?

The M-C-M' circuit, the goods matrix, and the question that follows

The M-C-M' Circuit

Capitalism is not "free markets." Markets existed for millennia. Capitalism is a specific circuit:

Simple exchange:
C → M → C
Sell linen, buy a coat. Finite. Satisfies a need.
Capital:
M → C → M'
Invest money, produce commodities, extract more money. No endpoint.

M' = M + ΔM (surplus value). The result is qualitatively identical to the start — just more money. So the process repeats. Forever. Capital is not a thing; it is value in motion that must perpetually expand.

"The circulation of capital has therefore no limits." — Karl Marx, Capital Vol. 1, Ch. 4
"Use-values must therefore never be looked upon as the real aim of the capitalist... The restless never-ending process of profit-making alone is what he aims at." — Marx, Capital Vol. 1

This limitless drive to accumulate — expand or die — is why capital inevitably bursts national boundaries and becomes imperialism.

The Four Types of Goods

Capitalism's M-C-M' circuit works best for one type of good:

↑ Non-Rivalrous
Public Goods
National defense, street lighting, open-source software, clean air, knowledge
Markets underprovide (free rider problem)
Club Goods
Netflix, toll roads, gyms, cinemas, encrypted WiFi
Require artificial scarcity (DRM, paywalls)
Common-Pool Resources
Fisheries, forests, groundwater, the atmosphere
Tragedy of the Commons (Hardin 1968)
Private Goods ★
Food, cars, housing, smartphones, oil
Capitalism's sweet spot
← Non-Excludable (Public) ↓ Rivalrous Excludable (Private) →

Capitalism's Blind Spots

  • Public goods (defense, knowledge, clean air) — no profit incentive, so markets underprovide them
  • Commons (fisheries, forests, atmosphere) — no excludability, so markets overexploit them
  • Club goods (Netflix, software) — require artificial scarcity (DRM, IP law) to generate profit from inherently non-rivalrous goods

M-C-M' only works where goods are rivalrous + excludable. As more of the economy moves into non-rivalrous territory (digital goods, knowledge, AI), the circuit breaks down.

Part I — Post-Capitalism

What Is Post-Capitalism?

Collective ownership • No state reliance • Better for everyone • No charity required

A post-capitalist mechanism must be:

  • Collectively owned without any state means — not state socialism, not charity, not custodial
  • Better than the capitalist alternative for both consumers and workers
  • Self-sustaining — structurally incentivized, survives without charity or custody

Example: Wikipedia

The canonical post-capitalist organization:

  • Collectively owned — no shareholders, no CEO, governed by community
  • Better for consumers — free, comprehensive, 300+ languages
  • No state involvement — Wikimedia Foundation is a nonprofit
  • Not charity — structurally self-sustaining through voluntary contributions

Example: The Mutual Vending Machine

A collectively-owned vending machine: the community owns the hardware and inventory. Profits return to the collective instead of a private operator. No landlord, no franchise fees, no corporate extraction. Lower prices for consumers, revenue funds community initiatives.

The same model applies to a mutual laundromat — collectively owned machines, cost-recovery pricing instead of profit-maximizing, surplus reinvested locally instead of extracted by distant investors.

What Is Imperialism?

A system of dispossession in the service of capital

Imperialism is "no less than a systemic dispossessive relation to the living and the life-making capacities of the colonized that operates in the debased service of the reproduction of capital in all its forms." — Neferti Tadiar, Remaindered Life (2022)
"It was the cable and telegraph, backed, of course, by sea power, that turned colonialism into imperialism: a system in which the center of an empire could dictate rather than merely respond to the margin." — James Carey, Communication as Culture (2009)
"Military action is the other necessary and unavoidable face of dollar imperialism." — Maurizio Lazzarato, War and Money
"Imperialism is the right of life and death over the population." — Lazzarato
"From the standpoint of capitalism... the violent appropriation of the colonial countries' most important means of production is a question of life or death for it." — Rosa Luxemburg, The Accumulation of Capital (1913)

Lenin's Four Features of Imperialism (1916)

  • Financialization Merging of bank and industrial capital into a financial oligarchy
  • Colonization Territorial division of the world among capitalist powers
  • Monopoly Concentration into monopolies dominating economic life
  • War Armed conflict as the mechanism for redividing territories

Capitalism requires ever-expanding non-capitalist frontiers to survive, but its method of incorporation destroys those frontiers. Imperialism is M-C-M' hitting the walls of the world.

Contemporary imperialism represents a "hyper" stage where digital platforms function as control infrastructure integrated with the military-intelligence apparatus — a "military-industrial-digital complex."

Why These Criteria?

Imperialism operates through the monopoly of violence (Lazzarato) and violent appropriation of means of production (Luxemburg). A post-imperialist mechanism must be fundamentally resistant to violence, coercion, and state capture. It must be antifragile — becoming more distributed and resilient in response to attacks, not merely surviving them.

Post-capitalism asks: "can we organize production differently?" Post-imperialism asks: "can we build infrastructure that empires cannot destroy?"

Utopian Socialism: A Brief History

Beautiful visions, fatal vulnerabilities

"Any general character, from the best to the worst, may be given to any community, even to the world at large, by the application of proper means." — Robert Owen, A New View of Society
"Under civilization poverty is born of superabundance itself." — Charles Fourier
"The golden age of the human race is not behind us, it is at the forefront." — Henri de Saint-Simon
"The Utopians attempted to evolve [solutions] out of the human brain... These new social systems were foredoomed as Utopian." — Friedrich Engels, Socialism: Utopian and Scientific (1880)

The Core Problem: Defense

Engels' critique: utopian socialists rejected class struggle, hoping to transform society through persuasion and model experiments.

But the deeper problem is defense. Every surviving utopian community did so by being too small to threaten existing power. When socialist projects posed a real threat — the Paris Commune (1871), revolutionary Spain (1936), Allende's Chile (1973) — they were crushed by military force.

Key Figures

  • Robert Owen (1771-1858) — Proved humane conditions + profitability at New Lanark. Founded New Harmony, Indiana (collapsed in 2 years). Lost 80% of his fortune.
  • Charles Fourier (1772-1837) — Proposed "phalanxes" of ~1,600 people. Proto-feminist. 25+ communities attempted, nearly all failed in 2-5 years.
  • Henri de Saint-Simon (1760-1825) — Envisioned governance by scientists and industrialists.

Beyond the Binary

If imperialism never achieves global hegemony, there are other options

The Key Assumption

The dialogue tree assumes you must either acquire weapons or submit. But this only holds if imperialism achieves total global hegemony. Historically, it never has. There have always been zones outside direct imperial control — ungoverned spaces, neutral territories, frontier regions.

The Expand / Recede Model

Instead of standing and fighting (acquiring nuclear arms) or submitting to an imperial patron, a network of autonomous collectives can expand into uncontested zones and recede from zones under imperial pressure.

  • No fixed territory to defend — the network is nomadic
  • Physical nodes can be agrarian communes, industrial cooperatives, or entire cities
  • When one node is threatened, assets and operations shift to others
  • The network grows by establishing new nodes, not by defending borders
  • Like water, it flows around obstacles rather than breaking through them

Insurance Against Imperialist Expansion

The network actively insures itself against imperial incursion:

1
zkTLS event verification — Cryptographically prove that a trusted news source published an article about invasion, sanctions, or martial law in a specific zone
2
Parametric trigger — When 3+ verified sources confirm the event, the insurance contract activates automatically
3
Asset liquidation — Automatically liquidates the collective's assets in that territory and converts to portable stablecoins
4
Relocation — Funds released by the zkTLS-verified trigger to relocate to a new zone

Example: Anti-Discrimination Insurance

LGBTQ+ members of a collective can pay a specific premium that automatically relocates them if gay marriage is banned in their territory. The zkTLS integration verifies the legislative change through trusted news sources, triggers the smart contract, and liquidates their local assets — no human intermediary, no bureaucratic delay.

A parallel economy that makes peace financially rational

Citizens of two conflicting nations (Country A & Country B) create a shared token economy. Cross-border trade earns bonuses. Aggression triggers token redistribution. Over time, enough economic activity shifts into the token system to reduce reliance on — and demand for — the aggressor state's fiat currency.

Dual-Token Model

  • Both tokens governed at the municipal level

Black Wednesday: How One Man Broke the Pound

September 16, 1992. George Soros identified that Britain joined the ERM at an overvalued rate. He built a $10 billion short position against the pound.

  • Risk-reward was asymmetric: max downside ~0.5%, upside 15-20%
  • Bank of England spent £27 billion defending — failed
  • Raised rates 10% → 12% → 15% in a single day — failed
  • UK withdrew from the ERM at 7:30 PM
  • Soros profited ~$1.8B. Pound fell 25% against the dollar.
  • Conservatives didn't return to power for 18 years.
Black Wednesday • Trade of the Century

Collectivizing the Mechanism

What if the same economic pressure could be applied by organized masses rather than one billionaire?

  • Morris & Shin (1998) proved coordinated attacks don't need explicit coordination. When participants share similar information, each independently attacks at the same threshold — a self-fulfilling equilibrium.
  • GameStop (2021) proved retail workers coordinating on forums can execute institutional-grade strategies without a central leader.
  • Terra/UST (2022) demonstrated a Soros-style attack on a crypto peg, destroying $45B in one week.

A Marxist Framework for Economic Resistance

This is not speculation — it is the logical extension of class struggle into the financial arena:

  • Identify the target: A nation-state with an overvalued, defensible-but-fragile currency peg
  • Collective signal: Public information (reserve ratios, trade deficits) replaces Soros's private conviction — available to all
  • Coordinated action: Smart contracts pool capital from thousands of participants, execute shorts, distribute gains collectively
  • Self-organizing: Per Morris-Shin, participants independently act on the same signal — no central command needed

On-chain transparency is the equalizer: publicly visible reserves and real-time data give every worker the intelligence that previously required being a billionaire.
