Part I — Post-Capitalism

What Is Capitalism?

The M-C-M' circuit, the goods matrix, and the question that follows

The M-C-M' Circuit

Capitalism is not "free markets." Markets existed for millennia. Capitalism is a specific circuit:

Simple exchange:
C → M → C
Sell linen, buy a coat. Finite. Satisfies a need.
Capital:
M → C → M'
Invest money, produce commodities, extract more money. No endpoint.

M' = M + ΔM (surplus value). The result is qualitatively identical to the start — just more money. So the process repeats. Forever. Capital is not a thing; it is value in motion that must perpetually expand.

"The circulation of capital has therefore no limits."— Karl Marx, Capital Vol. 1, Ch. 4
"Use-values must therefore never be looked upon as the real aim of the capitalist... The restless never-ending process of profit-making alone is what he aims at."— Marx, Capital Vol. 1

This limitless drive — expand or die — is why capital inevitably bursts national boundaries and becomes imperialism.

The Four Types of Goods

Capitalism's M-C-M' circuit works best for one quadrant:

↑ Non-Rivalrous

Public Goods

National defense, street lighting, open-source software, clean air, knowledge

Markets underprovide (free rider problem)

Club Goods

Netflix, toll roads, gyms, cinemas, encrypted WiFi

Require artificial scarcity (DRM, paywalls)

Common-Pool Resources

Fisheries, forests, groundwater, the atmosphere

Tragedy of the Commons (Hardin 1968)

Private Goods ★

Food, cars, housing, smartphones, oil

Capitalism's sweet spot

← Public↓ RivalrousPrivate →

Capitalism's Blind Spots

  • Public goods — no profit incentive, so markets underprovide
  • Commons — no excludability, so markets overexploit
  • Club goods — require artificial scarcity (DRM, IP law)

M-C-M' only works where goods are rivalrous + excludable.

What Is Post-Capitalism?

A framework for collective ownership beyond the state

A post-capitalist mechanism must satisfy all three:

✔ Collectively owned without any state means — not state socialism, not charity, not custodial

✔ Better than the capitalist alternative for both consumers and workers

✔ Self-sustaining — structurally incentivized, survives without charity or custody

"To change our economy we need to change our economic language, for the nature of our economy is bound by the expressivity of the language that can conceive it."— Bryan, Lopez & Virtanen, Protocols for Postcapitalist Expression
"Today, the main contradiction in modern capitalism is between the possibility of free, abundant socially produced goods, and a system of monopolies, banks and governments struggling to maintain control over power and information."— Paul Mason, PostCapitalism (2015)
"The real and controlling resource and the absolutely decisive 'factor of production' is neither capital, nor land, nor labor. It is knowledge."— Peter Drucker, Post-Capitalist Society (1993)
"The economic aspirations of a previous generation are no longer available to the majority of the population."— Akseli Virtanen, Towards Post-Capitalism

What Makes It Work

Yochai Benkler (Harvard Law) identified three requirements for successful commons-based peer production:

  • Modularity — tasks divisible into independent components
  • Granularity — variable sizes allowing different commitment levels
  • Low-cost integration — mechanisms for quality control

Wikipedia demonstrates that a non-market, non-state mode of coordination can outperform both firms and markets for certain types of production — especially knowledge goods.

The Caveat

Wikipedia is still "a prototype" (Bauwens) that cannot fully separate itself from capitalism — it relies on donations and infrastructure hosted within capitalist systems. But it proves the organizational model works at scale.

From Vending Machines to Cities

Post-capitalist mechanisms at every scale

The Mutual Vending Machine

A collectively-owned vending machine where the community owns the hardware and inventory. No private operator extracting profit.

  • Lower prices for consumers (no profit margin extracted)
  • Revenue stays local — funds community initiatives, not shareholders
  • Maintenance organized cooperatively by members
  • No landlord, no franchise fees, no corporate extraction

Scaling Up: The Mutual Laundromat

The same model, bigger:

  • Community owns or collectively leases the space
  • Machines purchased collectively, not leased from a corporation
  • Cost-recovery pricing instead of profit-maximizing
  • Users have direct stake in upkeep — no absentee owner neglect
  • Surplus reinvested locally or reduces member prices
  • Members vote on hours, pricing, improvements

A capitalist laundromat extracts ~30-40% profit margin. A mutual laundromat returns that to users.

What Is Imperialism?

A system of dispossession in the service of capital

Imperialism is "no less than a systemic dispossessive relation to the living and the life-making capacities of the colonized that operates in the debased service of the reproduction of capital in all its forms."— Neferti Tadiar, Remaindered Life (2022)
"It was the cable and telegraph, backed, of course, by sea power, that turned colonialism into imperialism: a system in which the center of an empire could dictate rather than merely respond to the margin."— James Carey, Communication as Culture (2009)
"Military action is the other necessary and unavoidable face of dollar imperialism."— Maurizio Lazzarato, War and Money
"Imperialism is the right of life and death over the population."— Lazzarato
"From the standpoint of capitalism... the violent appropriation of the colonial countries' most important means of production is a question of life or death for it."— Rosa Luxemburg, The Accumulation of Capital (1913)

Lenin's Four Features (1916)

  • Financialization Merging of bank and industrial capital
  • Colonization Territorial division of the world
  • Monopoly Concentration into monopolies
  • War Armed conflict for redivision
Lenin: Imperialism, Highest Stage of Capitalism

Luxemburg's Insight

Capitalism requires ever-expanding non-capitalist frontiers, but its method of incorporation destroys them. Imperialism is M-C-M' hitting the walls of the world.

"IMPERIALISM is the political expression of the accumulation of capital in its competitive struggle for what remains still open of the non-capitalist environment."

Modern Analysis

Contemporary imperialism is a "hyper" stage: digital platforms as control infrastructure integrated with the military-intelligence apparatus — a "military-industrial-digital complex."

Beautiful visions, fatal vulnerabilities

"Any general character, from the best to the worst, may be given to any community, even to the world at large, by the application of proper means."— Robert Owen, A New View of Society

The Core Problem: Defense

Every surviving utopian community did so by being too small to threaten existing power. When socialist projects posed a real threat — Paris Commune (1871), Spain (1936), Chile (1973) — they were crushed by military force.

  • Charles Fourier (1772-1837) — Phalanxes of ~1,600. Proto-feminist. 25+ communities, nearly all failed.
  • Henri de Saint-Simon (1760-1825) — Governance by scientists and industrialists.

The Key Assumption

The dialogue tree assumes: acquire weapons or submit. But this only holds under total global hegemony. Historically, there have always been zones outside direct imperial control.

The Expand / Recede Model

A network of autonomous collectives can expand into uncontested zones and recede from zones under imperial pressure.

  • No fixed territory — the network is nomadic
  • Physical nodes: agrarian communes, industrial cooperatives, or entire cities
  • When one node is threatened, assets shift to others
  • Grows by establishing new nodes, not defending borders
  • Like water, flows around obstacles
