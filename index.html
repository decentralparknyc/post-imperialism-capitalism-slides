Part I — Post-Capitalism

What Is Capitalism?

The M-C-M' circuit, the goods matrix, and the question that follows

The M-C-M' Circuit

Capitalism is not "free markets." Markets existed for millennia. Capitalism is a specific circuit:

Simple exchange:
C → M → C
Sell linen, buy a coat. Finite. Satisfies a need.
Capital:
M → C → M'
Invest money, produce commodities, extract more money. No endpoint.

M' = M + ΔM (surplus value). The result is qualitatively identical to the start — just more money. So the process repeats. Forever. Capital is not a thing; it is value in motion that must perpetually expand.

"The circulation of capital has therefore no limits."— Karl Marx, Capital Vol. 1, Ch. 4
"Use-values must therefore never be looked upon as the real aim of the capitalist... The restless never-ending process of profit-making alone is what he aims at."— Marx, Capital Vol. 1

This limitless drive — expand or die — is why capital inevitably bursts national boundaries and becomes imperialism.

The Four Types of Goods

Capitalism's M-C-M' circuit works best for one quadrant:

↑ Non-Rivalrous

Public Goods

National defense, street lighting, open-source software, clean air, knowledge

Markets underprovide (free rider problem)

Club Goods

Netflix, toll roads, gyms, cinemas, encrypted WiFi

Require artificial scarcity (DRM, paywalls)

Common-Pool Resources

Fisheries, forests, groundwater, the atmosphere

Tragedy of the Commons (Hardin 1968)

Private Goods ★

Food, cars, housing, smartphones, oil

Capitalism's sweet spot

← Public↓ RivalrousPrivate →

Capitalism's Blind Spots

  • Public goods — no profit incentive, so markets underprovide
  • Commons — no excludability, so markets overexploit
  • Club goods — require artificial scarcity (DRM, IP law)

M-C-M' only works where goods are rivalrous + excludable.

Part I — Post-Capitalism

What Is Post-Capitalism?

A framework for collective ownership beyond the state

Definition

In Post-Capitalist Society (1993), Peter Drucker argued that knowledge has replaced capital, land, and labor as the decisive factor of production. In a post-capitalist society, the basic economic resource is no longer capital or natural resources or labor — it is knowledge, and value is created by productivity and innovation applied by knowledge workers who own their own means of production.

✔ Collectively Owned

Without any state means — not state socialism, not charity, not custodial. A third mode of ownership distinct from both private property and state property.

✔ Better for Everyone

Better than the capitalist alternative for both consumers and workers. Lower prices, democratic control, intrinsic motivation over wage exploitation.

✔ Self-Sustaining

Structurally incentivized. Survives without charity or custody. Revenue comes from real value creation, not donations or state subsidies.

"To change our economy we need to change our economic language, for the nature of our economy is bound by the expressivity of the language that can conceive it."— Bryan, Lopez & Virtanen, Protocols for Postcapitalist Expression
"Today, the main contradiction in modern capitalism is between the possibility of free, abundant socially produced goods, and a system of monopolies, banks and governments struggling to maintain control over power and information."— Paul Mason, PostCapitalism (2015)
"The real and controlling resource and the absolutely decisive 'factor of production' is neither capital, nor land, nor labor. It is knowledge."— Peter Drucker, Post-Capitalist Society (1993)
"The economic aspirations of a previous generation are no longer available to the majority of the population."— Akseli Virtanen, Towards Post-Capitalism

What Makes It Work

Yochai Benkler (Harvard Law) identified three requirements for successful commons-based peer production:

  • Modularity — tasks divisible into independent components
  • Granularity — variable sizes allowing different commitment levels
  • Low-cost integration — mechanisms for quality control

Wikipedia demonstrates that a non-market, non-state mode of coordination can outperform both firms and markets for certain types of production — especially knowledge goods.

The Caveat

Wikipedia is still "a prototype" (Bauwens) that cannot fully separate itself from capitalism — it relies on donations and infrastructure hosted within capitalist systems. But it proves the organizational model works at scale.

A 3D-printable, community-owned vending machine network — mutual-vend.com

The Economics

A traditional vending machine generates ~20-30% net margin for a single owner. Mutual Vend distributes that margin across all participants:

  • Customers get revenue-share tokens (~2 week payout windows)
  • Stockers earn rewards for labor
  • Verifiers earn fees for honest attestation
  • Governance token (pegged 1:1 to USD) coordinates the network

The same model extends to mutual laundromats, mutual cafes, or any retail point. A capitalist laundromat extracts ~30-40% profit — a mutual one returns that margin to users as lower prices or shared revenue.

What Is Imperialism?

A system of dispossession in the service of capital

Definition

The term "imperialism" entered English around 1870, shortly after the laying of the transatlantic telegraph cable. Imperialism is the extension of a nation's power through territorial acquisition, economic domination, and military force. It is the political expression of capitalism's need to perpetually expand — the M-C-M' circuit bursting national boundaries in search of new markets, cheap labor, and raw materials. Where colonialism extracts resources from specific territories, imperialism is the system that coordinates that extraction globally.

Imperialism is "no less than a systemic dispossessive relation to the living and the life-making capacities of the colonized that operates in the debased service of the reproduction of capital in all its forms."— Neferti Tadiar, Remaindered Life (2022)
"It was the cable and telegraph, backed, of course, by sea power, that turned colonialism into imperialism: a system in which the center of an empire could dictate rather than merely respond to the margin."— James Carey, Communication as Culture (2009)
"Military action is the other necessary and unavoidable face of dollar imperialism."— Maurizio Lazzarato, War and Money
"Imperialism is the right of life and death over the population."— Lazzarato
"From the standpoint of capitalism... the violent appropriation of the colonial countries' most important means of production is a question of life or death for it."— Rosa Luxemburg, The Accumulation of Capital (1913)

Lenin's Four Features (1916)

  • Financialization Merging of bank and industrial capital
  • Colonization Territorial division of the world
  • Monopoly Concentration into monopolies
  • War Armed conflict for redivision
Lenin: Imperialism, Highest Stage of Capitalism

Luxemburg's Insight

Capitalism requires ever-expanding non-capitalist frontiers, but its method of incorporation destroys them. Imperialism is M-C-M' hitting the walls of the world.

"IMPERIALISM is the political expression of the accumulation of capital in its competitive struggle for what remains still open of the non-capitalist environment."

Modern Analysis

Contemporary imperialism is a "hyper" stage: digital platforms as control infrastructure integrated with the military-intelligence apparatus — a "military-industrial-digital complex."

Part III — The Defense Problem

Utopian Socialism: A Brief History

Beautiful visions, fatal vulnerabilities

Definition

Utopian socialism is a tradition of social thought that envisions an ideal society based on cooperation, equality, and communal ownership — achieved through peaceful persuasion and model communities rather than revolution. Unlike later "scientific socialism" (Marx/Engels), utopian socialists believed that demonstrating a better way of life would inspire voluntary adoption by people of all classes.

"Any general character, from the best to the worst, may be given to any community, even to the world at large, by the application of proper means."— Robert Owen, A New View of Society
"Under civilization poverty is born of superabundance itself."— Charles Fourier
"The golden age of the human race is not behind us, it is at the forefront."— Henri de Saint-Simon
"The Utopians attempted to evolve [solutions] out of the human brain... These new social systems were foredoomed as Utopian."— Engels, Socialism: Utopian and Scientific

The Core Problems: Defense & Scale

Every surviving utopian community did so by being too small to threaten existing power. When socialist projects posed a real threat — Paris Commune (1871), Spain (1936), Chile (1973) — they were crushed by military force.

Even when left alone, utopian communities could not scale. The personal trust and shared vision that held small groups together broke down as communities grew. Free-rider problems, governance disputes, and economic inefficiency prevented any secular commune from lasting beyond a few years or expanding beyond a few hundred members.

Key Figures

  • Robert Owen (1771-1858) — New Lanark success, New Harmony failure. Lost 80% of fortune.
  • Charles Fourier (1772-1837) — Phalanxes of ~1,600. Proto-feminist. 25+ communities, nearly all failed.
  • Henri de Saint-Simon (1760-1825) — Governance by scientists and industrialists.

Part IV — P2P Peace

P2P Sanctions

Citizens stake their own money on peace — enforced by code, not empires

How It Works

Citizens from both sides of a conflict stake their own money on specific sanctions. These sanctions are settled by an obfuscated aggregate of newspapers that both sides agree to in advance — verified cryptographically via zkTLS so no single source can be manipulated.

One Side Violates

The violating side loses their stake. The other side receives the payout. Citizens who bet on peace are rewarded; citizens whose government broke the peace pay the price.

Both Sides Violate

Both sides lose money. Staked funds are burned or redistributed to the peace pool. Mutual aggression is a Lose/Lose — there is no scenario where violence is profitable.

Peace Holds

Both sides get a payout from the accumulated pool of lost funds from past violations. The longer peace holds, the larger the reward. Peace compounds.

Settlement: The Newspaper Oracle

Sanctions are settled by an obfuscated aggregate of newspaper sources that both sides agree to before staking. Neither side knows the exact weighting or selection — only that verified, trusted sources are used.

  • zkTLS cryptographically proves articles came from verified publishers
  • Multiple sources must corroborate before triggering settlement
  • No single newspaper, government, or actor can manipulate the outcome
  • The oracle is trustless — it doesn't require trusting any individual party

What Makes This Different

  • Not enforced by imperialist violence or a state — enforcement is algorithmic, via smart contracts that execute automatically based on verified events
  • Higher-bandwidth democracy — ordinary citizens get direct power over military-level decisions through quadratic voting on sanctions, not just once-every-4-years elections
  • Programmable over centuries — sanctions can be coded to persist for hundreds of years, outlasting any single government, administration, or empire's attempt to destabilize them
  • Resistant to imperial manipulation — no single empire can unilaterally change, lift, or enforce sanctions for political gain

A Crypto Framework for Currency Pressure

1
Research & signal — Identify an overvalued peg. Publish on-chain analysis of reserve ratios, trade deficits, debt levels. Make the signal public.
2
Pool capital — Smart contract collects stablecoin deposits from participants. Each depositor receives proportional shares in the pool.
3
Execute via DeFi — Pool borrows the target currency on decentralized exchanges, sells it for stablecoins, creating sell pressure. Perpetual futures on DEXs (dYdX, Hyperliquid) amplify the position with leverage.
4
Distribute gains — When the peg breaks, the smart contract automatically buys back the depreciated currency, repays the loan, and distributes profits pro-rata to all participants.

Every step is transparent, auditable, and permissionless. No Soros required — just a smart contract and a public signal.
